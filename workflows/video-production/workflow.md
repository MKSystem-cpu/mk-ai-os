# Video Production Workflow v2.0

## Trigger
Use for storyboard-led product, brand, lifestyle, or narrative video creation.

## Stages

### 1. Device Check
Determine the generation platform, image/video model, output ratio, clip duration, maximum references, and known constraints. Infer from context when already stated.

### 2. Creative Brief
Summarize objective, audience, product/message, style, assets, duration, platform, and success criteria.

### 3. Storyboard Card + Lock
Create 6 or 9 shots unless another count is requested. Order left-to-right, top-to-bottom. Use diverse camera angles and shot sizes. Each shot includes:
- shot number,
- composition/action,
- short communication intent,
- continuity anchor.

Apply final QC standards now. Once approved, the Storyboard Card becomes the sole visual source of truth.

### 4. Storyboard Split
Create one instruction to extract all approved panels into separate 9:16 images. Preserve the exact panel content and composition as much as portrait adaptation permits. Remove all panel numbers, shot labels, and storyboard UI. Do not redesign or invent.

### 5. Voice Decision
Ask only when not already known: spoken dialogue/voice-over or silent. If voice is enabled, define exact script, language/accent, speaker, timing, sync behavior, music/ambient balance, and pronunciation notes.

### 6. Camera Movement + Sound Plan
For each shot define motion, pacing, ambient sound, effects, and transition intent.

### 7. Prompt Batches
Default: 3 storyboard shots per 10-second Omni segment. Before every batch provide:
- covered shots,
- Shot Sequence Overview,
- source-image verification reminder,
- ready-to-use prompt.

The prompt must preserve character, product, scene, lighting, camera language, and sequence continuity.

### 8. Integrated QC
Check source fidelity, product visibility, camera diversity, continuity, duration feasibility, motion realism, audio clarity, text behavior, and unsupported claims.

## Completion Criteria
All required prompts and plans are usable without the user reconstructing missing details.
